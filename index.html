Fox Step Privacy Policy

Effective date: May 12, 2026
App: Fox Step (com.foxstep.app)
Operator: Kay Dunn (senkaydunn@gmail.com)

Fox Step is a personal step and walk tracker. This policy explains what data the app collects, where it goes, and how to remove it.

What the app collects

  • Step counts from your phone's built-in step counter sensor.
  • Walk traces. When you start a walk, the app records GPS points until you tap End walk. Tracking runs as a foreground service with a persistent notification — never in the background without your knowledge.
  • Health Connect data, if you grant permission. The app reads step and exercise records to cross-validate the sensor count and backfill history. After a walk ends, the app can write the walk back to Health Connect as an exercise session.

Where the data lives

By default, everything stays on your phone in an encrypted local database.

If you sign in (optional, via an emailed magic-link code), the app syncs your steps, walks, and exercise records to a server at api.kaysplayground.com that Kay Dunn operates personally on Cloudflare Workers. The connection is HTTPS-encrypted. Without sign-in, no data leaves your device.

Who else sees the data

No one. The app shares no data with advertisers, analytics services, or any third party. Resend delivers the sign-in code email; the email contains only the six-digit code, no app data.

How long it's kept

  • On your phone: until you uninstall the app or delete entries yourself.
  • On the server (only if you signed in): until you delete your account. Account deletion uses a 30-day grace period — sign in again within that window to cancel deletion. After 30 days, the server wipes your data permanently.
  • In Health Connect: Health Connect manages its own storage. Uninstalling Fox Step does not remove records the app wrote to Health Connect.

How to delete the data

  • App data on your phone: uninstall, or use Settings → Diagnostics within the app.
  • Server data: Settings → Account → Delete account.
  • Health Connect entries: open the Health Connect app → Data → Exercise → remove Fox Step entries.

Security

Local data sits in a SQLCipher-encrypted database. The encryption key lives in the Android Keystore. All network traffic uses HTTPS.

Children

Fox Step is not intended for users under 13, in line with Health Connect's age requirement.
